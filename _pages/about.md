I keep coming back to a simple question: how does a brain become capable of behavior?

I work at the intersection of neural circuits and behavior, with a growing interest in how development shapes that relationship. I'm drawn to what behaviors are possible in the first place, and to how the structure of neural circuits constrains what an animal can actually do. My work spans systems — from rodent models to *Drosophila* — combining behavioral assays, circuit manipulations, and neural recordings. More recently, I've begun integrating a developmental perspective to study how circuits function and vary.

I enjoy the messy side of research: building things, trying ideas, and doing it alongside others. I mentor students, organize workshops and symposia, and contribute to a more open and connected scientific community. I care about how neuroscience is done — not just what we study, but how we work together to study it.
