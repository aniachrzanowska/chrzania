My scientific path wouldn't be the same without the people I met through the [Nencki Open Lab](https://nenckiopenlab.org/) and its initiatives.

Nencki Open Lab is an international network launched by neuroscientists from the Nencki Institute in Warsaw, now running in Warsaw, Cambridge, Heidelberg, Buenos Aires, and Paris. The goal is to create an environment where researchers can discuss their ideas, exchange thoughts with people from different subfields of neuroscience, and learn to use open tools to build their experimental setups and analyze their data. The initiative has two parts: the **School of Tools** (hands-on training in experimental neuroscience) and the **School of Ideas** (a space for confronting and discussing ideas).

Alongside the flagship events — like the [Transatlantic Behavioral Neuroscience School](https://nenckiopenlab.org/tbnss2026/) (held biannually between Argentina and Poland) and the School of Ideas in Neuroscience — we've so far run two Workshops of Ideas in Paris:

- [Decoding the brain: from methods to meaning](https://nenckiopenlab.org/decoding/)
- [Back to basics: what is a neuron?](https://nenckiopenlab.org/neuron/) — a new series.

We are preparing new things in Paris! If you're interested in organizing something together or just discussing, just [drop me a line](mailto:anna.chrzanowska@icm-institute.org).
